Real-Time Expression Recognition Prototype

Archie S. - April 15, 2018 8:00 am

Hello everyone. It’s been a long while, and that’s because I was busy with getting this prototype working. After much trial and error, I finally got something that worked…sort of. It’s still a prototype which means its accuracy is not ideal. For example, it has a bit of trouble recognizing the happy expression – often confusing it with sad. However, it’s pretty good at classifying the intended neutral and sad expressions correctly.

After researching a lot on OpenCV, I learned how to capture video data with it. After that, I had to integrate all my methods from my previous program into the new one. Essentially, the program is analyzing whatever face it sees frame-by-frame. This is a lot of work, however, due to the efficiency of the LBPH method, the computer has no trouble in terms of lag time. Since it takes only a few seconds to create an LBPH face recognizer, I scrapped my initial plan to store the recognizer in data files and have it referred back to whenever needed. Perhaps I will need to do this when I start more rigorous trials with more training data. However, I’m still only using 9 images for a person (3 neutral, 3 happy, 3 sad).
